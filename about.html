I’m Dominick Weston, a student of computer science and Japanese at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. This site is a place for me to document and distill my thinking—whether through essays, projects, or personal notes.

My interests include philosophy of mind, political theory, and systems-level programming. I’m particularly drawn to questions that resist easy answers, and I tend to approach both code and ethics with the same principle: clarity through constraint.

I use this site to refine my voice, share my work, and record the arc of thought as it shifts over time. If something here challenges you—or clarifies something unspoken—then it’s done its job.
